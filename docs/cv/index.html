[def:tri] A right-angled triangle is a triangle whose sides of length \(a\), \(b\) and \(c\), in some permutation of order, satisfies \(a^2+b^2=c^2\).

The triangle with sides of length \(3\), \(4\) and \(5\) is right-angled.

This lemma follows from [def:tri] since \(3^2+4^2=9+16=25=5^2\).

[thm:py] Triangles with sides of length \(a=p^2-q^2\), \(b=2pq\) and \(c=p^2+q^2\) are right-angled triangles.

These are all pretty interesting facts.
